Wing Bristles


Here you can find the count of bristles in a selected region of the wing.


Bristles veins of Drosophila are ideal for studying the generation of spatial patterns. Because most mutations affect the bristle count, the drosophila wing bristle is regarded as the most crucial component of the investigation.

Counting the wing bristles of Drosophila melanogaster can provide important information about the genetics of these insects and how it may be related to human genetics. The number and arrangement of wing bristles in Drosophila melanogaster is genetically determined and can be used as a marker to identify specific genetic mutations or defects. By counting the wing bristles, researchers can identify any changes in the normal pattern of bristle arrangement that may be caused by genetic mutations or other factors, such as exposure to certain medications or environmental conditions.
