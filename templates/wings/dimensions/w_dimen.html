Wing Analysis and Measurement


Drosophila wings are known for their unique structure, with a characteristic pattern of veins and other features that make them an important model for genetic and developmental studies. The wings consist of five longitudinal veins, two cross veins, and various other structures such as trichomes and hairs that are distributed across the surface and edge of the wing.

These features provide a visual representation of the activity of conserved signaling pathways that are relevant to human health and disease. By analyzing the structure of Drosophila wings, researchers can gain insights into the genetic and developmental processes that underlie organ development, growth, and tissue regeneration.



The Drosophila Wing Analysis and Segmentation module is a powerful tool within our web application, designed to provide users with a comprehensive analysis of Drosophila wings. This module enables users to upload images of Drosophila wings and obtain detailed information on the dimensions of the wings, including area and perimeter measurements, and individual segment dimensions.
